FAQ

How long should each LinkedIn experience bullet be?

Aim for 100-180 characters per bullet, or roughly 18-28 words. The 40-profile audit found that profiles passing Item 3 averaged 23 words per bullet. Anything under 60 chars reads as placeholder; anything over 220 gets skimmed past on mobile, where most recruiter screens happen.

Does LinkedIn render bullet markers (•) properly?

Yes — unicode bullet markers render in the experience description across desktop, mobile web, and the iOS/Android apps. LinkedIn does not parse markdown (no - or * hyphens converting to bullets), so unicode is the reliable path. The combined block this tool produces uses • followed by a space.

Should I write in first person ("I shipped X") or third person ("Shipped X")?

Third person, past simple. Convention on LinkedIn experience bullets matches resume convention — "Shipped", "Led", "Reduced". The audit found that profiles using "I" or "my" in bullets felt off-brand to recruiters scanning quickly. The About section is where first person is appropriate; the experience section is not.

What if I don't have hard numbers for every bullet?

Estimate with named scope instead. "Led migration affecting all 12 backend services" is more credible than "Led a complex migration project". If you have no numerical scope at all on a given bullet, the score will flag it — usually that signals the bullet should be combined with another or dropped entirely. The audit found 3-5 metric-bearing bullets outperformed 7-8 vague ones.
